# Claims and Limits

APL Sidecar is an experimental local-first runtime. Its evidence covers the APL-controlled workflow, not a provider's internal systems.

## What APL demonstrates

- payloads prepared and recorded by configured adapters;
- outputs used by the local workflow;
- canonical character-based disclosure measurement;
- local-only field fingerprints;
- internal receipt consistency and Ed25519 signature verification;
- an exploratory reconstruction assessment under its stated method.

## What APL does not prove

- provider deletion, non-retention, or absence of separate logs;
- impossibility of intent reconstruction;
- non-collusion between providers;
- removal of all semantic information;
- cryptographic secrecy of task intent;
- guaranteed privacy, anonymity, compliance, or legal non-liability;
- compatibility with every model, SDK, or provider.

The phrases “zero leakage,” “zero exposure,” “fully anonymous,” “completely private,” “provider sees nothing,” and “no trace” are prohibited as unqualified product claims.
