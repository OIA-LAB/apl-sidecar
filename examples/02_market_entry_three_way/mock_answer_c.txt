Standard launch risks for consumer hardware entering retail:

1. First to bite (weeks 1-8): inventory mis-allocation between regions and
   DOA/returns spikes — both visible in sell-through and RMA rate almost
   immediately. Certification gaps surface here too if any market was
   assumed rather than verified.

2. Second wave (months 2-4): review-driven PR risk (one viral defect
   thread outweighs ten good reviews), logistics cost creep on small
   replenishment orders, and warranty-reserve underestimation.

3. Early metrics that expose them: weekly sell-through vs forecast, RMA
   rate vs category norm, support-ticket first-response backlog, and
   replenishment order size trend.

4. Minimal cadence for a small team: weekly 30-minute risk stand-up for
   the first quarter (sell-through, RMA, tickets), dropping to biweekly in
   the second quarter if all three metrics hold inside thresholds.
